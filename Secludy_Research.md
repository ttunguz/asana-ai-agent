# Research: Secludy

## Overview

Secludy is a synthetic data startup that focuses on generating privacy-guaranteed synthetic data. Their primary value proposition is eliminating the risk of leaking sensitive Personally Identifiable Information (PII) when training AI models. They target enterprise AI teams that require high security and performance.

**Key Features:**

*   **Privacy Guarantee:** 99.99% privacy and IP leakage proof.
*   **Technology:** Uses Differential Privacy (DP) to fully anonymize data.
*   **Compliance:** Data is out of scope for regulations like GDPR, CCPA, and HIPAA.
*   **Testing:** Includes Canary PII leakage tests and membership inference attack detection.
*   **Deployment:** Offers self-hosting in private clouds or on-premise, with integrations for AWS, Azure, GCP, and Databricks.

## Competitive Landscape

The synthetic data market is crowded. Here's a look at some of the other players and how they position themselves:

*   **Gretel.ai:** Developer-focused platform for creating safe, anonymized data.
*   **MOSTLY AI:** Enterprise platform for data unlocking, sharing, and simulation.
*   **Syntho:** AI-based engine to generate data that mimics statistical characteristics.
*   **Hazy:** Focuses on privacy-preserving, high-quality data for ML and analytics.
*   **Datagen:** Specializes in photorealistic images and videos for computer vision.
*   **Syntegra:** Focuses on the healthcare vertical.
*   **K2view:** Offers end-to-end management of the synthetic data lifecycle.

## Secludy's Differentiation

Secludy's main differentiator is its **unwavering focus on eliminating regulatory risk through the application of differential privacy**. While other startups also prioritize privacy, Secludy has built its brand around the "privacy-guaranteed" concept, backing it up with specific security features. This messaging is designed to resonate with large enterprises where data privacy and compliance are paramount.

In contrast, other startups in the space may have a broader focus on:

*   **Data quality and fidelity.**
*   **Specific data types (e.g., images, videos).**
*   **The developer experience.**
*   **End-to-end data management.**
